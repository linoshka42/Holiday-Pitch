The Pitch

Why the Philippines
beats Goa this Christmas

Like Goa, the Philippines was shaped by Catholic colonialism — but while Goa's Christmas is a warm nostalgic echo, the Philippines turns it up to eleven. Pasko (Christmas) begins in September and doesn't stop until January. Streets fill with parols (star lanterns), dawn masses ring out at 4am, and families feast through the night of Noche Buena. It's Goa's Christmas — amplified across 7,641 tropical islands, with world-class beaches, whale sharks, and zero crowds knowing it's the best-kept festive secret in Asia.

🪴 Goa (The Usual)

  • Familiar but predictable — same beach, same restaurants
  • December sees tourist crowds and higher prices
  • Christmas limited to small Catholic pockets of Old Goa
  • Beach options mostly concentrated in one stretch
  • Nightlife focus can overshadow family atmosphere

🌴 Philippines (The Upgrade)

  • 90% Catholic nation — Christmas is a national obsession
  • World's longest Christmas season — entire country is decorated
  • Noche Buena, Simbang Gabi masses, parol festivals
  • 7,641 islands to explore: El Nido, Boracay, Cebu, Siargao
  • Warm, dry December weather — peak season for island-hopping
10 Reasons to Say Yes
⛪

Most Catholic Christmas in Asia

With 90% of the population Catholic, Christmas is woven into Filipino identity. Every street, mall, and village is lit with parols from September through January.

🕯️

Simbang Gabi — Nine Dawn Masses

From Dec 16–24, churches hold 4am masses followed by street food stalls selling bibingka and puto bumbong. It's one of Asia's most magical living traditions.

🏮

Giant Lantern Festival, Pampanga

San Fernando — the "Christmas Capital of the Philippines" — hosts a dazzling festival where villages compete with 20-foot illuminated parol lanterns.

🍽️

Noche Buena Feast on Christmas Eve

Midnight Christmas Eve dinner is sacred — lechon (roasted pig), pancit, bibingka, and queso de bola. It's Goa's Christmas dinner but louder, warmer, and wilder.

🏝️

El Nido & Palawan in Peak Season

December is the best month to visit Palawan. Calm seas, no rain, crystal visibility — perfect for island hopping through limestone karst lagoons.

🦈

Swim with Whale Sharks

Oslob in Cebu offers whale shark encounters year-round. December is one of the best months — add it to the itinerary for a truly unforgettable morning.

🎉

Boracay New Year's Eve

White Beach in Boracay hosts one of the world's great New Year countdown parties — beachside fireworks, music, and the whole island celebrating together.

💰

Exceptional Value

World-class food, accommodation, and activities at Southeast Asian prices. Island hopping, beachfront dinners, and fresh seafood are remarkably affordable.

😊

The Warmest People on Earth

Filipinos are regularly ranked among the world's most hospitable and joyful people. Travelling here during Christmas — their favourite time of year — is something else entirely.

✈️

Accessible for Everyone

Direct or one-stop flights available from all your cities — London, Dubai, Muscat, Singapore, Goa, Melbourne, and Florida. Manila's NAIA is a major Asian hub.

December 24 – January 2

10 Days, 3 Islands,
One Unforgettable Christmas

This itinerary moves through three distinct worlds — the festive Catholic heart of Manila, the untouched paradise of El Nido, Palawan, and the legendary beaches and New Year atmosphere of Boracay. Internal flights are short and cheap. The pace allows for spontaneity without any rushed moments.


Approximate total in-country cost per person: $600–900 USD (excl. international flights), covering accommodation, food, activities and inter-island flights. Budget options available throughout!

Manila ✈ El Nido ✈ Boracay

✦ ~10 days · Perfect loop

24
Dec 24 · Christmas Eve
Manila
🎄 Noche Buena
Arrival & Simbang Gabi
  • ●Arrive Manila (NAIA). Check in to hotel in BGC or Makati
  • ●Evening: Midnight Mass at the historic Manila Cathedral in Intramuros
  • ●Noche Buena feast — lechon, pancit, bibingka at a family Filipino restaurant
  • ●Experience the streets come alive with parols and carollers
💡 Book a restaurant for Noche Buena well in advance — this is the biggest meal of the Filipino year.
25
Dec 25 · Christmas Day
Manila
🎄 Maligayang Pasko!
Intramuros, History & Festive Cheer
  • ●Morning Christmas Day Mass at San Agustin Church (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • ●Explore Intramuros — Manila's 16th-century Spanish walled city
  • ●Kalesa (horse carriage) ride through the old town
  • ●Visit Rizal Park and the National Museum
  • ●Evening: Ayala Triangle Festival of Lights in Makati — spectacular light show
💡 The Festival of Lights runs hourly from 6pm — arrive early for the best spot.
26
Dec 26
Manila → El Nido
Fly to El Nido, Palawan
  • ●Morning: Fly Manila → El Nido (Air Juan or Cebu Pacific, ~1hr 20min)
  • ●Check into beachfront accommodation in El Nido town
  • ●Afternoon: Recover at Las Cabañas Beach — best sunset in El Nido
  • ●Evening: Fresh seafood dinner on the waterfront
💡 Book El Nido flights at least 6 weeks ahead — they sell out fast in peak season.
27
Dec 27
El Nido, Palawan
Island Hopping Tour A — The Lagoons
  • ●Full-day island hopping: Big Lagoon, Small Lagoon (kayak), Secret Lagoon
  • ●Stop at Shimizu Island for snorkelling with sea turtles
  • ●Floating lunch on the boat — fresh grilled fish, rice, tropical fruit
  • ●Seven Commandos Beach for the afternoon
💡 Tour A is the classic — book through your resort or a local operator for ~₱1,500pp ($27).
28
Dec 28
El Nido, Palawan
Bacuit Bay & Hidden Beaches
  • ●Tour C: Helicopter Island, Star Beach, Snake Island sand bar
  • ●Cliff jumping at Pinasil Island (optional, for the brave)
  • ●Afternoon: free time — explore El Nido town, visit local craft stalls
  • ●Sunset cocktails at Trattoria Altrove or Happiness Beach Bar
29
Dec 29
El Nido → Boracay
Travel Day — Fly to Boracay
  • ●Morning flight: El Nido → Manila → Caticlan (~3hrs total with connection)
  • ●Speed boat transfer to Boracay island (10 min)
  • ●Check in to White Beach resort — D'mall area for maximum access
  • ●Evening: First walk along White Beach — 4km of powder-fine sand
💡 Boracay is car-free — everything is within walking distance on White Beach.

Dec 30
Boracay
Beach Day & Water Sports
  • ●Morning: Paraw sailing (traditional outrigger) at sunrise
  • ●Snorkelling, cliff diving at Ariel's Point (full-day tour)
  • ●Afternoon: Puka Shell Beach on the north end — quieter, rawer
  • ●Sunset cocktails at any of the beach bars along White Beach Station 2

  • ●NYE countdown on White Beach — one of Asia's great New Year parties
  • ●Midnight fireworks over the sea, music, dancing, the whole beach celebrating
💡 Filipinos take New Year fireworks very seriously — the whole island lights up. Embrace it!
