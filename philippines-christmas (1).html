The Pitch

Why the Philippines
beats Goa this Christmas

Like Goa, the Philippines was shaped by Catholic colonialism — but while Goa's Christmas is a warm nostalgic echo, the Philippines turns it up to eleven. Pasko (Christmas) begins in September and doesn't stop until January. Streets fill with parols (star lanterns), dawn masses ring out at 4am, and families feast through the night of Noche Buena. It's Goa's Christmas — amplified across 7,641 tropical islands, with world-class beaches, whale sharks, and zero crowds knowing it's the best-kept festive secret in Asia.

🪴 Goa (The Usual)

  • Familiar but predictable — same beach, same restaurants
  • December sees tourist crowds and higher prices
  • Christmas limited to small Catholic pockets of Old Goa
  • Beach options mostly concentrated in one stretch
  • Nightlife focus can overshadow family atmosphere

🌴 Philippines (The Upgrade)

  • 90% Catholic nation — Christmas is a national obsession
  • World's longest Christmas season — entire country is decorated
  • Noche Buena, Simbang Gabi masses, parol festivals
  • 7,641 islands to explore: El Nido, Boracay, Cebu, Siargao
  • Warm, dry December weather — peak season for island-hopping
10 Reasons to Say Yes
⛪

Most Catholic Christmas in Asia

With 90% of the population Catholic, Christmas is woven into Filipino identity. Every street, mall, and village is lit with parols from September through January.

🕯️

Simbang Gabi — Nine Dawn Masses

From Dec 16–24, churches hold 4am masses followed by street food stalls selling bibingka and puto bumbong. It's one of Asia's most magical living traditions.

🏮

Giant Lantern Festival, Pampanga

San Fernando — the "Christmas Capital of the Philippines" — hosts a dazzling festival where villages compete with 20-foot illuminated parol lanterns.

🍽️

Noche Buena Feast on Christmas Eve

Midnight Christmas Eve dinner is sacred — lechon (roasted pig), pancit, bibingka, and queso de bola. It's Goa's Christmas dinner but louder, warmer, and wilder.

🏝️

El Nido & Palawan in Peak Season

December is the best month to visit Palawan. Calm seas, no rain, crystal visibility — perfect for island hopping through limestone karst lagoons.

🦈

Swim with Whale Sharks

Oslob in Cebu offers whale shark encounters year-round. December is one of the best months — add it to the itinerary for a truly unforgettable morning.

🎉

Boracay New Year's Eve

White Beach in Boracay hosts one of the world's great New Year countdown parties — beachside fireworks, music, and the whole island celebrating together.

💰

Exceptional Value

World-class food, accommodation, and activities at Southeast Asian prices. Island hopping, beachfront dinners, and fresh seafood are remarkably affordable.

😊

The Warmest People on Earth

Filipinos are regularly ranked among the world's most hospitable and joyful people. Travelling here during Christmas — their favourite time of year — is something else entirely.

✈️

Accessible for Everyone

Direct or one-stop flights available from all your cities — London, Dubai, Muscat, Singapore, Goa, Melbourne, and Florida. Manila's NAIA is a major Asian hub.

Eat Your Way Through the Philippines

Filipino Food —
A Christmas Feast & Beyond

Filipino cuisine is a glorious collision of Spanish, Chinese, Malay and American influences — bold, rich, unapologetically flavourful. December is the best time to eat here: Noche Buena tables groan with lechon and bibingka, street BBQ stalls fire up every evening, and halo-halo cools you down under the tropical sun. From Anthony Bourdain's "best pig ever" to Jollibee's legendary Chickenjoy — there's something for everyone, at every budget.

Main Dish
Lechon
The king of the Filipino table
Whole roasted pig, slow-turned over charcoal for hours until the skin shatters like glass and the meat melts. Anthony Bourdain called Cebu lechon "the best pig I've ever had." The centrepiece of every Noche Buena feast — you will understand the hype.
🔥 Must-try in Cebu 💰 ~₱350–500 per portion
National Dish
Chicken Adobo
The soul of Filipino cooking
Chicken (or pork) slow-braised in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves and black pepper until deeply savoury and falling off the bone. Every household has their version. Served with garlic fried rice, this is the dish that will follow you home.
⭐ Everywhere 💰 ~₱80–150
Street Food
Pork BBQ Skewers
Inasal-style grilled pork on a stick
Marinated in a sweet-savoury soy-calamansi glaze and grilled over charcoal, these skewers are the Philippines' ultimate street snack. Found at every corner, market and beachside stall. Get three, hand over ₱30, and watch the sunset. Simple perfection.
🍢 Beach stalls & markets 💰 ~₱10–15 per stick
Bar Classic
Sisig
Sizzling chopped pork — the ultimate pulutan
Chopped pig's cheek, ears and liver seasoned with calamansi, chilli and onions, served sizzling on a cast-iron plate. Invented in Pampanga, now beloved nationwide. The crackling, charred edges are the best part — order it with a cold San Miguel.
🍺 Best with cold beer 💰 ~₱150–250
Comfort Food
Sinigang
Tamarind sour soup — the Filipino hug
A bright, tangy broth made with tamarind and loaded with pork ribs (or prawns or fish), kangkong, eggplant and long beans. Sour, savoury, deeply comforting. Voted one of the world's best soups. One spoonful and you'll understand why Filipinos call it home.
🥣 Every Filipino restaurant 💰 ~₱150–280
Christmas Table
Pancit
Noodles that bring long life
Stir-fried rice or glass noodles with vegetables, shrimp, pork and soy sauce. A non-negotiable at every Filipino celebration — noodles symbolise long life. You'll eat this at Noche Buena on Christmas Eve alongside the lechon. Don't cut the noodles. Bad luck.
🎄 Christmas Eve essential 💰 ~₱80–180
Slow Cooked
Kare-Kare
Rich oxtail stew in peanut sauce
Slow-braised oxtail and tripe in a thick, golden peanut sauce with banana blossom, eggplant and long beans. Always served with bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste) whose pungency cuts brilliantly through the richness. A dish you'll be thinking about for days.
🥜 Pampanga origin 💰 ~₱250–400
Dessert Icon
Halo-Halo
"Mix-mix" — the Philippines' beloved chaos dessert
Shaved ice drenched in evaporated milk, topped with sweet beans, jackfruit, coconut jelly, leche flan, ube ice cream and whatever else is going. It's a beautiful mess. Translates as "mix mix" — which is exactly what you do. The perfect antidote to December heat.
🍧 Best at Milky Way Café, Manila 💰 ~₱80–150
Christmas Only
Bibingka
The unmistakable scent of Christmas morning
Soft, eggy rice cake baked in banana leaves with salted egg and grated coconut. Sold exclusively outside churches during the Simbang Gabi dawn masses (Dec 16–24). The smell of bibingka cooking on charcoal at 4am is one of the great sensory experiences of Filipino Christmas.
⛪ Outside churches at dawn 💰 ~₱50–80
Christmas Only
Puto Bumbong
Steamed purple rice — Christmas in a bite
Deep violet sticky rice steamed inside bamboo tubes, served with butter, grated coconut and muscovado sugar. Vivid purple, slightly chewy, faintly sweet. Only appears at Christmas time alongside bibingka. The colour alone makes it feel like a festival food from another world.
💜 Dec 16–24 only 💰 ~₱40–70
Street Snack
Lumpiang Shanghai
The party spring roll — always on the Noche Buena table
Thin, crispy deep-fried spring rolls packed with seasoned pork and vegetables. A staple at every Filipino party and Christmas table. Dipped in sweet chilli sauce, these disappear faster than they're made. Order extra — you will regret if you don't.
🎄 Noche Buena staple 💰 ~₱5–10 each
Grill House
Chicken Inasal
Bacolod-style lemongrass grilled chicken
Chicken marinated in lemongrass, ginger, calamansi and annatto, then grilled over charcoal and basted in seasoned butter. The Bacolod version is the original and still the best — juicy, smoky, slightly tart. Eat with garlic rice and a cup of the drippings for dipping.
🍗 Best in Bacolod, also Mang Inasal 💰 ~₱100–180

🐝 Jollibee — The Philippines' Beloved Fast Food King

No food guide to the Philippines is complete without Jollibee. This homegrown fast food chain beats McDonald's here and has a near-religious following. The Chickenjoy — crispy fried chicken with gravy — is legendary. Try the spaghetti with sweet Filipino-style meat sauce. It's an experience unlike anything you've had before, and you'll find one in every city, airport, and town across the islands.

  • 🍗
    Chickenjoy — Crispy fried chicken, the icon. ~₱89
  • 🍝

🍌 Eat on Banana Leaf

Order a Boodle Fight — all dishes spread on banana leaves, eating with your hands. The ultimate communal Filipino dining experience. Ask any resort or restaurant to arrange one.

🥤 Drinks to Try

San Miguel Pale Pilsen (the local beer), fresh buko (coconut water), calamansi juice, and taho — warm silken tofu with syrup and tapioca pearls sold by street vendors each morning.

🛵 Street Food Safety

Busy stalls with high turnover are your best bet. Grilled skewers, fresh lumpia and bibingka from outside churches are all safe and delicious. Avoid anything that's been sitting out long.

💜 The Ube Obsession

Ube (purple yam) flavours everything — ice cream, pastries, pancakes, cake. It's sweet, earthy and uniquely Filipino. Try ube halaya (jam) and ube pandesal (purple bread rolls) for breakfast.
